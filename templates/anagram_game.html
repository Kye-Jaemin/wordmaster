About anagram puzzles

An anagram rearranges the letters of a word to form a different arrangement. The puzzle here is the reverse: you see the letters jumbled, and your job is to reconstruct the original word.

Why this matters for learning: anagrams force you to engage with spelling at the letter level. You can't shortcut by recognizing a word's shape — you have to genuinely know how it's spelled. That makes anagrams an underrated tool for moving vocabulary from passive recognition (you'd know it if you saw it) into active production (you can write it from memory).

Pair anagram and the Tile Guess format on the same word and you cover both halves of vocabulary mastery: shape recognition + letter-level spelling.
