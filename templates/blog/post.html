{% if slug == "wordle-tips" %}

Whether you're a casual player or a hardcore word-puzzle enthusiast, these strategies will help you consistently beat WordMaster in fewer guesses.

1. Start with High-Frequency Letters

The most common letters in 5-letter English words are: E, A, R, I, O, T, N, S, L, C. A great opening word covers as many of these as possible.

Best openers: CRANE, STARE, AUDIO, RAISE, ARISE

2. Your Second Guess Should Eliminate New Letters

Don't waste your second guess repeating letters you already know. Choose a word that tests 4–5 completely new letters. If your first was CRANE, try STOMP or DULY.

3. Watch for Double Letters

Many words have repeated letters (SLEEP, TEETH, LLAMA). If you're stuck, consider that the same letter might appear twice.

4. Use Yellow Tiles Aggressively

A yellow tile tells you the exact letter is in the word — just not in that position. Try it in every other position before giving up on it.

5. Common Endings to Try

If you have 3 letters fixed, consider these endings: -TION, -NESS, -MENT, -IGHT, -OUND, -IGHT.

{% elif slug == "best-starting-words" %}

The starting word can make or break your game. After analyzing thousands of word games, here are the top starting words ranked by letter coverage.

Tier 1: The Best Starters

  • CRANE — Covers C, R, A, N, E (5 high-frequency letters)
  • STARE — S, T, A, R, E — excellent vowel + consonant balance
  • AUDIO — Hits 4 of 5 vowels in one shot
  • RAISE — R, A, I, S, E — covers the most common letters

Tier 2: Strong Alternatives

  • ARISE, ATONE, ROAST, STALE, TRACE

Two-Word Opening Strategy

Some players use a fixed two-word opener: CRANE + STOMP covers 10 unique letters and gives you a huge head start by guess 3.

{% elif slug == "word-game-history" %}

Word guessing games have a surprisingly rich history — long before the internet brought them to millions of players every morning.

The Newspaper Era

Crossword puzzles originated in the early 1900s in American newspapers. By the 1950s, word puzzles were a daily ritual for millions of readers. "Jotto" — a paper-and-pencil code-breaking word game from 1955 — is considered an early ancestor of today's digital word games.

The Digital Revolution

The 1980s and 90s saw word games migrate to personal computers and early gaming consoles. Scrabble, Boggle, and similar games found new audiences through digital adaptations.

The Viral Era (2021–Present)

In late 2021, a simple daily word game created as a gift went globally viral, inspiring thousands of variants and establishing the "daily challenge" format as a new genre. Today, word guessing games attract hundreds of millions of players worldwide.

Why Are They So Addictive?

Psychologists point to several factors: the satisfaction of pattern recognition, the social element of shared daily challenges, and the healthy sense of competition that comes from comparing results without revealing the answer.
